About Jamia Ahli Hadees Mohammadia Masjid Cherwan

A beacon of faith, knowledge, and community service in the heart of Cherwan

Affiliation

Jamia Ahl-e-Hadees Mohammadia Masjid Sharif is proudly affiliated with Jamiat Ahl-e-Hadees Jammu & Kashmir, Barbarshah. The masjid is managed by Jamiat Ahl-e-Hadees Unit Cherwan, which oversees its religious, educational, and welfare initiatives. This affiliation ensures alignment with the broader mission of promoting Islamic knowledge and community service.

History

The vision of establishing a spiritual and educational hub in Cherwan was brought to life by a group of devoted individuals committed to faith and community service. Under the leadership of Khursheed Ahmad Mir, son of Ali Mohammad Mir and then President of Jamiat Ahl-e-Hadees Unit Cherwan, the foundation stone (Sang-e-Buniyad) of the masjid was laid. The initial development of the masjid was overseen by Manzoor Ahmad Magaray, who served as Chairman during this pivotal phase. His dedication played a crucial role in transforming the vision into reality.

First Prayer and Azan
On the historic day of April 28, 2018, the foundation stone was laid, and the first Asr Salah was offered. The first Azan (call to prayer) was given by Mohtaram Manzoor Ahmad Magray, marking a new spiritual era for the community.

Investments & Assets

The masjid owes its existence to the generosity of local benefactors who donated land for this noble cause. Their selflessness laid the foundation for a place that continues to serve the community.

Primary Land Donors:


1. Gh. Qadir Sheer Gojri (Son of Gh. Mohammad Sheer Gojri)
2. Abdul Raheem Sheer Gojri (Son of Ab Salam Sheer Gojri)

With time, as the masjid's influence and responsibilities grew, additional land was acquired, expanding the total area to approximately 19 marlas. This expansion has enabled the masjid to accommodate more worshippers and enhance its educational and social welfare programs.

Financial Management

Transparency and integrity are at the heart of the masjid's financial operations. Tr. Mohammad Yousuf Mir ensures that all financial resources are managed efficiently and utilized for the betterment of the community.

Social Welfare Services

Beyond its spiritual role, the masjid, through the efforts of Jamiat Ahl-e-Hadees Unit Cherwan, has been at the forefront of community welfare. Through Darul Mawasat Bait-ul-Maal, several welfare programs are carried out to support the needy and vulnerable.

Welfare Programs:
1. Marriage Assistance for Orphans - Providing financial aid and logistical support for orphan girls' weddings.
2. Employment Generation Programs - Creating job opportunities for the unemployed.
3. Medical Aid for Chronic Patients - Offering financial assistance and medicines to those suffering from long-term illnesses.
4. Disaster Relief for Flood Victims - Providing emergency assistance to those affected by floods and other natural calamities.
5. Food Assistance for Poor Families - Ensuring underprivileged families receive essential food supplies.
